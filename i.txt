## 1. PCA Interpretation

Principal Component Analysis was applied after standardizing the features to reduce dimensionality and understand variance in the dataset.

From the PCA summary:

* **PC1 explains 8.38% variance**
* **PC2 explains 8.11% variance**
* **PC3 explains 7.91% variance**
* **PC4 explains 7.14% variance**

The **first 10 principal components explain about 63% of total variance**, while the **first 16 components explain around 91%**.

**Interpretation**

This indicates that the variance in the mobile phone dataset is spread across many variables. No single component dominates the structure of the data, suggesting that multiple features contribute to the differences between mobile devices.

---

## 2. Scree Plot Interpretation

The scree plot shows the percentage of variance explained by each principal component.

Observation:

* The variance decreases gradually from PC1 onward.
* There is no sharp drop after the first few components.

**Interpretation**

Since the curve declines smoothly, it suggests that the dataset does not have a very strong low-dimensional structure. Several components are required to represent the variability of the data.

---

## 3. PCA Biplot Interpretation

The PCA biplot displays both **observations (mobile phones)** and **feature vectors**.

Key observations:

* Variables pointing in the **same direction are positively correlated**.
* Variables pointing in **opposite directions are negatively correlated**.
* Variables near the origin contribute less to the first two principal components.

From the plot:

* **RAM, battery power, and mobile weight** point in a similar direction, suggesting a positive relationship.
* **Front camera (fc) and primary camera (pc)** also show strong directional alignment, indicating correlation.
* Network-related variables like **3G and 4G** are positioned similarly, suggesting similar behaviour.

**Interpretation**

Phones with larger RAM and higher battery power tend to cluster together in the PCA space, indicating that these features contribute significantly to device differentiation.

---

## 4. Elbow Method Interpretation

The elbow plot shows the **Total Within Sum of Squares (WSS)** for different numbers of clusters.

Observation:

* WSS decreases sharply from **k = 1 to k = 4**.
* After **k = 4**, the decrease becomes gradual.

**Interpretation**

The elbow occurs at **k = 4**, indicating that **four clusters** provide a good balance between model simplicity and clustering accuracy.

Therefore, **K-Means clustering with 4 clusters was selected**.

Using PCA and K-Means clustering, the mobile phone dataset was grouped into **four distinct clusters based on hardware specifications and performance features**.
